# The Last Garden on Mars

Mars was cold, silent, and covered in iron dust. Humans lived safely inside glass domes powered by machines. Real plants had nearly disappeared.

A young girl named Lyra discovered an old seed hidden inside her grandmother’s necklace. Attached to it was a message:

"When the last flower blooms, Mars will live again."

Lyra secretly planted the seed in a forgotten corner of the colony. Every day she carried tiny cups of water across the station and whispered stories to it.

Weeks passed.

Then one morning, a single silver flower bloomed beneath the red Martian sky.

The colony scientists were amazed. The flower released oxygen stronger than any machine could produce. Soon more flowers were planted, and green life slowly spread across Mars.

Years later, forests grew beneath giant glass skies, and children played beside rivers for the very first time.

People called Lyra “The Gardener of Mars,” but she always smiled and said:

“It only needed someone to believe it could grow.”

# The Prince and the Paper Crown

Long ago, there was a young prince named Leo who wanted to be the greatest king in the world. He wore a heavy golden crown and gave grand speeches, but the people were not happy.

One rainy day, Leo saw a poor boy making paper crowns for children in the street. The children laughed and danced, wearing the simple crowns as if they were treasure.

“Why do they love your paper crowns more than my golden one?” asked the prince.

The boy answered, “Because I make them smile.”

Leo took off his golden crown and sat with the children. He helped fold paper crowns until the rain stopped and the sun returned.

When Leo became king, he ruled with kindness instead of pride. And every year, on the first day of spring, he wore a paper crown to remember that a true ruler serves with joy.
